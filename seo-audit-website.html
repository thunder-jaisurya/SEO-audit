What to check in an SEO audit

A strong audit covers technical health, page relevance, content quality, internal linking, and authority. Start with blockers first, then move toward growth opportunities.

Technical SEO

Check crawlability, indexing, broken pages, redirects, XML sitemaps, canonical tags, JavaScript rendering, mobile usability, and Core Web Vitals.

On-page SEO

Review titles, meta descriptions, heading structure, URL slugs, keyword targeting, search intent match, image alt text, and semantic page structure.

Authority & trust

Measure backlink quality, referring domains, brand mentions, topical authority, EEAT signals, testimonials, business trust pages, and competitor link gaps.

Things that help a website rank

Ranking improvements usually come from aligning intent, improving technical quality, increasing topical depth, and building authority around the pages that matter most.

1
Pick one clear keyword intent per page.

Do not make several pages compete for the same search query unless they serve clearly different search intent.

2
Improve titles and opening sections.

Your title tag, H1, intro, and subheadings should match what the searcher is trying to solve.

3
Build topic clusters.

Create supporting pages that link back to your main commercial or pillar page so authority flows through the site.

4
Fix page speed and mobile UX.

Compress large images, reduce render-blocking resources, and make sure layouts are stable on slower devices.

5
Earn quality backlinks.

Digital PR, partner pages, case studies, directories, citations, and linkable assets are safer than spammy link building.

Simple ranking workflow

Use a repeatable process so your team always starts with the highest-impact fixes before producing more content or buying more tools.

Step 01

Find blockers

Check indexing, redirects, broken pages, duplicate content, canonicals, sitemap coverage, and major performance regressions.

Step 02

Map keywords to pages

Match each high-value keyword group to a target URL. Merge, redirect, or rewrite pages that overlap too much.

Step 03

Improve content quality

Add missing sections, comparisons, FAQs, visuals, examples, and internal links so the page is better than competing results.

Step 04

Promote winners

When a page starts moving up, support it with backlinks, refreshes, stronger anchors, and better CTR optimization.

Quick audit priorities

If you only have a few hours, start with pages that already get impressions. Those pages usually produce the fastest ranking gains after better titles, content updates, and UX fixes.

High priority

Index coverage issues, noindex mistakes, broken canonicals, duplicate versions, redirect loops, and pages that are excluded from search unexpectedly.

Medium priority

Weak title tags, missing FAQs, thin sections, poor internal links, outdated content, and pages with declining click-through rate.

Growth priority

New topic clusters, digital PR campaigns, linkable assets, comparison pages, and high-intent landing pages for new service lines.
